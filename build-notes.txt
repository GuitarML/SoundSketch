

1. Trim the backside midi and expression solder pin joints, they are too tall to fit potentiometers on top. 
   Put potentiometer covers on the bottom of the potentiometers, or they will short out the midi and expression jack leads.

2. Solder potentiometers. Install board into enclosure with potentiometers and switches in place, and tighten jack nuts. 
   This will ensure everything is set properly before soldering. Solder the 6 potentiometers (BUT NOT THE TOGGLE SWITCHES YET).

3. Remove the pcb and inspect. 

4. Solder toggles. VERY IMPORTANT: APPLY ELECTRICAL TAPE TO OUTSIDE OF TOGGLE BLOCKS, OTHERWISE WILL SHORT
   OUT ON THE SIDES OF THE POTENTIOMETER LEADS. Install toggles on pcb, then put pcb into enclosure, tightening
   everything in place (including 4 audio/exp/midi jacks) as with the potentiometers to ensure proper fit. 
   Solder the 3 toggle switches.

5. Solder the 2 LEDs on the pcb. Trim LED leads as neccessary, testing fit into LED bezels before soldering. 
   Solder LEDs onto the pcb and test fit in enclosure. 

6. Solder the power jack wires to the pcb with pcb not installed in enclosure. There is not much room
   in the enclosure with the pcb installed so this makes it easer to solder the power wires. 

7. Install pcb back into enclosure, then fit power jack and power jack nut into enclosure. There is not much room
   between the power jack nut and pcb. Solder the power jack wires to the power jack leads. 

8. Solder the 4 momentary footswitch wires to the momentary footswitches before installing into the enclosure.
   Its easier to do this first. Then install the footswitches into the enclosure and solder footswitch wires to the pcb.

9. Tighten all nuts. Visually inspect and check for anything loose. 

10. Install Daisy Seed and test.
